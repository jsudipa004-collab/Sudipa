Global Awareness & Ethical Considerations

Sustainability in Practice

EcoBrick Initiative — Environmental Engineering Ethics

  • Waste Reduction as Engineering: The EcoBrick project demonstrated that engineering solutions can emerge from everyday materials — reframing plastic waste not as a problem but as a resource to be designed around.
  • Community-Centred Design: Effective environmental solutions require public awareness and behavioural change, not just technical intervention. Outreach is as important as the solution itself.
  • Responsibility Beyond the Lab: Engineers have an obligation to consider the social and environmental lifecycle of any material or process they work with.
Industrial Ethics

Safety & Sustainability in Industrial Processes

  • Safety as a Design Constraint: Exposure to large-scale industrial facilities reinforced that safety protocols are non-negotiable design parameters, not afterthoughts.
  • Sustainable Scale: Observing operations at Amul, IFFCO, and Expresolv highlighted the environmental footprint of industrial-scale production and the importance of sustainability planning.
  • Ethical Manufacturing: Quality assurance and regulatory compliance are ultimately about protecting end-users and communities — not just passing inspections.

Core Belief: Chemical engineering carries a profound responsibility — the processes we design affect ecosystems, communities, and future generations. I commit to approaching every problem with both technical rigour and environmental conscience.
